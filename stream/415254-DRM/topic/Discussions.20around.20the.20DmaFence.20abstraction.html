Boris Brezillon (Feb 10 2026 at 08:44):

So, drivers emit dma_fences that are exposed to the outside world. Those fences are usually attached to buffers (GEM objects, or dma_buf objects) through their dma_resv. The fences in dma_resv::fences are automatically evicted every time the buffer idleness is checked. But, there are cases where no one checks for idleness for a long period of time. This means that fences emitted by drivers, even if they are signaled, can stay around, and block the driver unloading.
