bjorn3 (Jul 04 2021 at 21:27):

re running doctests in the kernel instead of the host: that is not possible even with nightly features. rustdoc builds and runs every test directly. It does not allow building and then letting the user run the tests. In addition every doctest is a separate executable. This would mean a separate kernel image for each test.

Miguel Ojeda (Jul 04 2021 at 21:29):

Yeah, the idea is to change the compiler to support our use case, i.e. pick all functions marked as test etc. and list them to be executed at e.g. boot time (or however selftests/kunit does it). It will also require a test crate that is not dependent on std, etc.

Miguel Ojeda (Jul 04 2021 at 21:32):

In the beginning, of course, we can write a kernel-only, independent tool that does it, based on the rustc_* libs or just a compiler fork -- we do not need it right away, and anyway it is only for testing, which already has extra requirements on the toolchain, like the hacks to convince cargo -Zbuild-std to do what we want etc.

bjorn3 (Jul 04 2021 at 21:40):

Yeah, #[test] is already doable (especially on nightly, I believe I already linked to the testing chapter of phil-opp's blog os) It is just rustdoc tests that are much harder.

Miguel Ojeda (Jul 04 2021 at 21:47):

Ah, I see -- then probably a fork of rustdoc is the best approach, no? And then when we get it sufficiently in good shape, discuss it with rustdoc folks etc.

bjorn3 (Jul 04 2021 at 22:16):

Maybe. Rustdoc depend on internal compiler api's. It requires changes like once every week or every other week to keep building I think. This is much more often than liballoc. Also building it requires the rustc-dev rustup component containing rlibs for all the crates of which rustc consists, which I am not sure is shipped for stable.

Miguel Ojeda (Jul 04 2021 at 22:27):
