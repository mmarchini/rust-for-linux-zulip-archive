Daniel Almeida (Oct 29 2025 at 00:55):

We can discuss this on a call if needed but tl;dr is: you only get a Bound device as the argument of some specific functions, like probe for example

Daniel Almeida (Oct 29 2025 at 00:56):

Or anywhere else where the kernel knows that the device is actually bound. Otherwise you have to use the more expensive “try_access()” method on Revocable instead of “access()”

Daniel Almeida (Oct 29 2025 at 00:57):

Read up on Rust typestates btw

Daniel Almeida (Oct 29 2025 at 00:57):

try_access works with any &dev::Device

Daniel Almeida (Oct 29 2025 at 00:59):

You’ll need to add new read/write variants that use try_access underneath. Btw try naming them try_read and try_write
