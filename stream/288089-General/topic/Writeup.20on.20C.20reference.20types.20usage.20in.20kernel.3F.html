Oliver Mangold (Dec 04 2024 at 07:19):

I can tell what experience we had at work, where we also have need for documentation of a similar kind. We started out with Mediawiki, which is quite pleasant to work with, at least at the beginning, as it has lots of formatting options and plugins for that purpose. Efficient to edit, as it is almost WYSIWYG and doesn't need anything but a browser.

But after a couple of years parts of the crew became unhappy with it, because it is a not particularly convenient to track changes. Thus we moved to documenting in RST, putting the files in a repo in Gitlab and make the Gitlab CI auto-rebuild these to html which is published through Gitlab Pages. Certainly less messy, but you need Git and Sphinx locally installed and run a rebuild to see how the change looks.

Not sure what would be better, but an argument in favor of RST might also be, that when parts of the docs are stabilized they could be upstreamed into the main kernel documentation.
