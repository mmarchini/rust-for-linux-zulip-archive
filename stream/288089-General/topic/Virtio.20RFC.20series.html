Manos Pitsidianakis (May 21 2026 at 06:58):

Hello all, I have posted a series introducing abstractions for the virtio subsystem. The patches have since evolved since I abandoned the sample virtio-rtc driver for a reference virtio-rng driver (which also adds bindings/abstractions for the hw_random subsystem)

Latest RFC series, out of date:

https://lore.kernel.org/rust-for-linux/20260510-rust-virtio-v3-0-1427f14d67e1@pitsidianak.is/

In-progress tree:

https://github.com/epilys/linux/tree/rust-virtio

I have a few process questions:

  • Since this work touches on more than one subsystem at once, should I split the series per subsystem or put them together? For example virtio and hw_random
  • Can I introduce abstraction code that an existing driver doesn't use? I'd think and say no, it's not good to have dead code.
  • I've mailed the hw_random maintainers to let them know as suggested by the rust-for-linux docs, no reply yet. If I get no reply I will proceed with posting the patches on LKML. Is that okay?
  • My virtqueue abstraction is inherently unsafe (though in the patches I sent weeks ago they are not marked as so, I plan to change that.) Is it okay to publish unsafe abstractions and add safe abstractions on top of them later? (In particular I plan to introduce "owned" virtqueues on top of the unsafe virtqueue).
