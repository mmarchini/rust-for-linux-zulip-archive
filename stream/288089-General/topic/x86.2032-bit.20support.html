Trevor Gross (Jan 23 2024 at 01:04):

Is there anything special needed to get 32-bit x86 working, or just a build system patch? Since aarch64, loongarch, and RISC-V are all merged or close to it, it seems worth getting that working too https://docs.kernel.org/rust/arch-support.html

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 23 2024 at 01:50):

x86 requires -Zregparm=3 support and -Zreg-struct-return too -- see https://github.com/rust-lang/rust/issues/116972 and https://github.com/rust-lang/rust/issues/116973. The former is the blocker, since the latter can be done through target.json if needed.

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 23 2024 at 01:58):

By the way, in case someone wants to take a look: regparm should have been just a bit of plumbing in rustc, but it turns out Clang is the one that has the logic to decide the registers, which then get marked in LLVM IR, and thus sadly it requires to copy-paste the logic into rustc somehow (or convincing the LLVM project to move it into LLVM and then wait a long time).
