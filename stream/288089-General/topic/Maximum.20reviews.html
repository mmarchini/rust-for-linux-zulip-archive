Vincenzo Palazzo (Sep 21 2023 at 09:27):

In my humble opinion, there is no such thing as a pointless patch (like this conversation is); everyone has to start somewhere. As is often the case in all projects, simple patches tend to receive more reviews. Perhaps reviews should skip over the simple patches if they have already been reviewed? because at this point the review is pointless not the patch. If you are fixing this code in a patch series it should be in its own commit so the trivial patch is always present.

The 'Review-by' tag also serves to inform kernel developers. It signals that I am familiar with the changes, so if something goes wrong and breaks things, I know how to fix it. In this case, having more than one 'Review-by' might seem redundant from a review perspective.

However, it's not redundant from the author's perspective because this process teaches them how to contribute to the Rust subsystem. I am aware that the USB subsystem has different rules regarding patch title formatting and other aspects and also a simple patch that fix a typo can teach something to someone (e.g: if you are not familiar with English, this process teaches you in what form write your commit).
