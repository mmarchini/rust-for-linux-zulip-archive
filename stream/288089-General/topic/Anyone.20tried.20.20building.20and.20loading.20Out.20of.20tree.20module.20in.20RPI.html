Miguel Ojeda (Jan 30 2026 at 20:37):

did run make LLVM=1 rustavailable , pi said Rust is available! without any errors.

Note that that only means your Rust toolchain is available, not that you can enable CONFIG_RUST.

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 30 2026 at 20:47):

By the way, before trying out-of-tree modules, I would suggest first trying to get CONFIG_RUST enabled in your kernel plus e.g. rust_minimal (one of the trivial samples), then booting and seeing if you can see its output in the kernel log. Then, when that works, you can try other bits more easily, including out-of-tree modules.

@Akshai M Yeah, I meant that example. And yeah, when someone says "Rust enabled kernel" they usually mean CONFIG_RUST=y. If that is not set, as Gary says, you will not have any Rust (and no Rust out-of-tree modules can be built either).

In the menuconfig line you pasted above, I see e.g.:

That means it will not be possible to enable -- either you disable MODVERSIONS or you enable GENDWARFKSYMS.
