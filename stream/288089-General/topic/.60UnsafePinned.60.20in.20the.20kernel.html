Mitchell Levy (Apr 18 2025 at 17:34):

@Boqun Feng this is very interesting for the pcpu work, since it allows "This means, in particular, that &mut UnsafePinned is not necessarily a unique pointer, and thus the compiler cannot just make aliasing assumptions. "

Boqun Feng (Apr 18 2025 at 17:36):

Yeah, but it's not a free ticket, if you provide a public API, you will have to provide a Pin<&mut UnsafePinned>, because &mut UnsafePinned alone is not immune to swap().

Boqun Feng (Apr 18 2025 at 17:55):

One of the usage of UnsafePinned is that you already some T : Sync (i.e. having interior mutability), but for a few special functions you want to provide exclusive accesses (only one thread can do it at a time), then you can provide a function foo(Pin<&mut T>) where T is a UnsafePinned. This can make sure 1) no concurrent foo() called while foo() being called* and 2) compiler won't assume nolias when foo() being called, so a co-existing &T is not a UB.

*) The API has to guarantee no co-existing Pin<&mut T>.
