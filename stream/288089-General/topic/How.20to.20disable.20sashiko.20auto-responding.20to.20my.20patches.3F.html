Danilo Krummrich (Apr 23 2026 at 00:50):

Just to clarify, I'm not saying this is a policy issue, but a technical limitation, which can go both directions -- a subsystem that does not want contributors to receive those mails could also disable it for those who want them when multiple lists are addressed.

In general my experience has been that sashiko can provide very valuable feedback. However, one reason why a subsystem might not want to have mails sent to the contributor is that it might lead to the contributor acting on feedback without further discussion / involvement from everyone else the patch is addressed at.

One possible way to address this could be an explicit note in replies that mentions something along the lines of "If this report did provide feedback you consider acting on, please raise the topic on-list first.".

Gary Guo (Apr 23 2026 at 11:13):

As long as it could find actual bugs (with reasonable false positives rate), people would want to use it to improve quality of codebase. I don't think pushing to have maintainers to verify the output of Sashiko instead of sending it to the author is a good thing, given that they're already busy and overworked.

You don't ask maintainers to verify that Clippy is happy with your code. It has it's fair share of false positive too. Sashiko is just another tool like this.

Perhaps false positive rate of Sashiko is a bit too high today, but that should be the direction of improvement rather than a complete dismissal.

Gary Guo (Apr 23 2026 at 11:23):

FWIW, a lot of time I spent last cycle reviewing was pointing out things that will be easily caught by AI review tool by not otherwise available by existing deterministic tools. Missing inline, wrong import format, missing invariant comment, etc. It would save everyone's time if that sort of issues are reported and fixed early.

Perhaps you patch series has a big version number so it doesn't have many of these issues now; but I think having them enabled for v1/v2 would be a net benefit.
