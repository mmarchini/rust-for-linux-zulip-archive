Topic: Argument-position const generics

Gary Guo (Oct 10 2022 at 13:57):

I am currently advocating argument-position const generics (https://rust-lang.zulipchat.com/#narrow/stream/219381-t-libs/topic/atomic.3A.3AOrdering.20as.20a.20const.20generic), and I'd like to know about your thoughts.

For IoMem currently we just take a supposed-to-be-const-but-not-marked-as-such parameter and use build_assert! to check that it's within bound. We could alternatively change that into a const parameter but that'll look like base.readb::<GPIODIR> instead of base.readb(GPIODIR). I am purposing that Rust could add argument-position const generics so we can write the latter but behaves as the former.

I am personally not fond of base.readb::<GPIODIR>, what's your feel towards that syntax?

Asahi Lina (Oct 12 2022 at 17:52):

Not sure if this is something you already had in mind, but as an aside: with more complex hardware, it is very common to have to compute register offsets at runtime (e.g. indexing into an array of registers, or reading register indices from hardware variant descriptions), so in those cases they can't be const.

Benno Lossin (Oct 12 2022 at 17:54):

Jubilee (Oct 12 2022 at 23:06):

My thoughts are that we experimented with it before and it's largely a misfeature. It provides confusing errors, the only existing implementation is unforgivably hacky, and the syntactic distinction revealing that you are using a const generic argument helps visually distinguish such arguments, since they have special requirements on them.
