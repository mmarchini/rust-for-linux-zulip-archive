Topic: Explicit type conversions

Andreas Hindborg (Oct 31 2024 at 13:07):

At some point we decided pointer conversions of the form as _ was a bad idea because they make some code changes that probably should break the build silent. At that point we decided to use ptr::cast instead. @Tamir Duberstein and I are discussing how to use ptr::cast. Should we prefer ptr::cast<explicit_type>() or the less verbose ptr::cast()? The first option is more robust in the face of refactoring, while the latter is more easy on the eye.

Opinions?

Tamir Duberstein (Oct 31 2024 at 13:09):

Note that cast already differs from as _: the latter can also cast constness, the former cannot.

Alice Ryhl (Oct 31 2024 at 13:48):

We use .cast() lots of places. It's fine.

Andreas Hindborg (Oct 31 2024 at 13:56):

Us using it in a lot of places does not make it fine. If you mean it as two separate statements, the first statement is potentially an issue and the second one lacks justification.
