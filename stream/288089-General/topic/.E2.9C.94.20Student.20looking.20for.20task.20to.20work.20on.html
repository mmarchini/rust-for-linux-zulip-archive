Trevor Gross (Jul 11 2024 at 01:53):

Usually when somebody works on a new subsystem / driver type, they first mimic an existing C driver (so you have something to test against and a proof of concept), and then write one in Rust for something new that will actually get upstreamed. Duplicates of existing C drivers don't get upstreamed (very few exceptions exist), nor do bindings that don't have an in-kernel driver using them.
