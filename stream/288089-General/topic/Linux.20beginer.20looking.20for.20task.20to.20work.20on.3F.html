Alice Ryhl (Jul 25 2024 at 08:11):

The main barrier for getting involved is that to get a driver merged, it must be a driver that the relevant Linux kernel maintainer actually wants. Most of the time, this means that it needs to be a new driver that the kernel doesn't already have. I don't have a suggestion for a driver, unfortunately.

Alice Ryhl (Jul 25 2024 at 08:12):

As for your PR, sending PRs on github isn't how code gets merged. When Rust was accepted into the kernel in ... I think it was late 2022, we switched to using the Linux kernel mailing list instead of github.

Trevor Gross (Jul 25 2024 at 20:02):

I suggested the PR just as a place where it's easy for someone to give you some general feedback without noise on the list - merging PRs doesn't happen anymore. And as the others have stated (and I previously mentioned), everything that gets merged needs an in-tree usecase and really can't be a duplicate of a C driver. In other words, abstractions really can't be merged if the only thing they support is sample code.

I'll still take a look at the PR once I get the chance, just to help you get an idea of the shape things usually take. Which I think is all you are looking for. Weren't you considering making it work on top of rust-next?

As far as what to do next, I don't really know of anything one-off projects that have a chance of getting upstreamed (though you could look around the non-rust lists and see if you anything interesting). If you are interested in tty, maybe you could do work getting the relevant abstractions up to date so that they work on top of rust-next? Presumably they'll be needed by something at some point
