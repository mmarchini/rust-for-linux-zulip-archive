Hey Lina,

We were discussing what to do with the rust branch a couple days ago. There are two options, either we freeze it more or less (perhaps modulo fixes that somebody needs/wants), or we minimize the differences as much as possible with rust-next, i.e. making it get closer and closer to upstream as more things get upstreamed to mainline. Miguel Ojeda (Jan 22 2023 at 02:23):

I was just wondering about whether it was the original authors expected to do the work for everything or whether other people are helping with picking things out for upstreaming too.

For things that were/are not yet merged into rust (e.g. open PRs), the original authors (i.e. we typically would ping them to please submit it as a patch to the mailing list).

For things in the rust branch, if something has not had modifications on top, ideally the original authors too, but in general for core stuff things are intertwined with modifications on top of other modifications over time, so we are submitting the final result instead (like in the original merge, which is why it had the many Co-developed-bys etc.). In other words, we are not "replaying" the history of the rust branch upstream.
