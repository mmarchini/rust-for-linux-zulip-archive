Topic: `CoherentAllocation` without `As/FromBytes`?

Alexandre Courbot (Nov 10 2025 at 14:25):

Hi everyone,

Currently CoherentAllocation can only wrap types that implement both AsBytes and FromBytes. I understand that as a requirement of the current allocation flow, since memory is allocated and then returned without initialization, thus requiring that any byte pattern is valid.

However, what if we had a new constructor that took an impl Init to properly initialize the allocated memory before it is returned? Could we then relax the requirement for AsBytes and FromBytes? Or are these other reasons to keep them - possibly the fact that we don't trust the device with which that memory is shared?

I am asking that because we have a few unsafe impl As/FromBytes ... in Nova that are due to this and which I would love to eventually get rid of.

Alice Ryhl (Nov 10 2025 at 14:26):

Do you mean that the structs do not actually satisfy the safety requirements of the traits?

Alice Ryhl (Nov 10 2025 at 14:27):

Because IMO the long term solution is to derive the traits so that it's not unsafe. We should not remove them because they operate to/from raw bytes.

Alexandre Courbot (Nov 10 2025 at 14:27):

They do so far (in our case they are mostly arrays of u32), but indeed there might be a case in the future where they don't.

Alexandre Courbot (Nov 10 2025 at 14:28):

Beyond the practical aspect (a derive macro would also work great), I am also curious about the possibility to lift that requirement by making sure the allocated memory is correctly initialized via an Init

Alice Ryhl (Nov 10 2025 at 14:29):

The memory may be modified by the device.

Alexandre Courbot (Nov 10 2025 at 14:29):

Right, so we don't trust the device here

Alice Ryhl (Nov 10 2025 at 14:30):

At the very least, if you want to trust the device, you need to unsafely assert so somewhere. I would personally suggest that we don't go that route, though.

Andreas Hindborg (Nov 11 2025 at 08:43):

Either any bit pattern must be valid, or you need to use a MaybeUninit. We could implement the conversion trait for any MaybeUninit.

Alice Ryhl (Nov 11 2025 at 10:52):

Alice Ryhl (Nov 11 2025 at 10:52):

Just use structs of integer types.
