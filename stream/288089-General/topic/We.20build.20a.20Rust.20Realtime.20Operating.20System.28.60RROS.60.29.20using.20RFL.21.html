Topic: We build a Rust Realtime Operating System(`RROS`) using RFL!

Hongyu Li (Dec 12 2023 at 09:13):

Hello,

We are a research group from the Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT) in China. Over the past two years, we have been utilizing Rust for Linux (RFL) to develop a real-time kernel named RROS. This kernel operates alongside the Linux kernel in a dual kernel architecture, a concept introduced by Xenomai/EVL. RROS leverages the dovetail patch from Xenomai/EVL for dual kernel functionality and is compatible with Xenomai/EVL's user library, libevl. Essentially, RROS is a Rust-based version of Xenomai/EVL, incorporating interfaces from the RFL safety abstraction.

We have implemented RROS on the RPI 4b and utilized it as a satellite payload. Following the successful launch of our satellite, we are thrilled to announce the official open-sourcing of RROS, available here.

However, RROS is still a work in progress and faces several challenges:

  1. The presence of numerous unsafe blocks in RROS requires significant time to address.
  2. We encounter a lack of necessary APIs in RFL, leading us to create custom, albeit imperfect, wrappers (bindings::foo). Developing proper safety abstractions will take time.
  3. The dovetail patch, which deeply modifies the Linux kernel, is far from being integrated into the mainline. Consequently, RROS will remain an out-of-tree driver. We are aware of the documentation regarding out-of-tree drivers. This means our safety abstractions cannot be reviewed for correctness by the community, and the APIs we depend on may change over time.

Despite its imperfections, we still want to share RROS with the community, showcasing our serious commitment to using RFL for substantial projects and contributing to the community's growth. Our development journey with RROS has been greatly enriched by the support and knowledge from the RFL community. We also have received invaluable assistance from enthusiastic forks here, especially when addressing issues related to safety abstraction. We extend our heartfelt thanks to everyone involved!
