Matthew Maurer (Dec 15 2025 at 23:03):

I can tell you that Android managed to bootstrap and collide a rustc built through mrustc with one built through a rustc downloaded from rust-lang.org, with a procedure at https://cs.android.com/android/platform/superproject/main/+/main:prebuilts/rust/bootstrap/

We've been building our Rust toolchain forwards from that one since.

Since I know folks who are skeptical of rust-lang.org downloads will probably skeptical of stuff from Google too, they can either:

  1. Manually do what I did above. It takes a bit of fiddling, but there should be less nondeterminism to get lucky with these days.
  2. Use guix, which has put a lot of effort into maintaining a minimal binary seed, which does not use a prebuilt rustc (or even a prebuilt gcc or clang!). https://bootstrappable.org/ may be a good resource for understanding this, but users can also just install guix if they want to get a rustc that they know does not depend on any prebuilt binary from rust-lang.org

Ben Wright (Dec 27 2025 at 19:31):

Prebuilt binaries have always had this problem. Imagine for instance you are delivering chrome binaries to users across the world or windows ota updates. If the signature is from the publisher and you have the respective cert it is implicitly trusted. One area in this space that has been worked on pretty extensively is the concept of Binary Transparency which uses a public ledger to validate that you received the same binary everyone else has you can read about it more here:

  • https://binary.transparency.dev/
  • https://security.googleblog.com/2023/08/pixel-binary-transparency-verifiable.html

If you want to go a level beyond that you can double down with reproducible builds:

  • https://reproducible-builds.org/docs/deterministic-build-systems/

In this model you have various sources reproducing the same builds and writing to their ledger the hash that they observed allowing you to distribute trust authority.

For compiler chains that goes all the way back to Ken Thompson in (https://wiki.c2.com/?TheKenThompsonHack) and you would need a trusted chain from the original builds all the way up to counter that.
