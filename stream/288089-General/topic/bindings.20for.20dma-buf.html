It needs to be done either way when sending one of the pieces upstream, indeed, but maintaining the branch is still some more work, because it needs taking care of both sides rather than one (e.g. new changes to Rust stuff landing in mainline directly, removing outdated things in rust, back merging LInus' branch (i.e. new changes to non-Rust stuff), etc.).

Miguel Ojeda (Feb 02 2023 at 19:37):

On your side (Andreas), you will still need to do merging/rebasing to keep track of the newer rusts, and do yet more rebases at the end to prepare the final patches to submit to rust-next etc.

What maintaining the rust branch allows us (and you if you keep merging/rebasing on top of it) is to see what is left more clearly, and for you to move back and forth from rust to mainline when you get closer, etc.
