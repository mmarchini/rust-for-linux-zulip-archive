Topic: ✔ Opaque: Does it guarantee no writes after drop()

Philipp Stanner (Jun 18 2026 at 08:34):

I've got a rather special problem. I have described it in detail here.

Basically:
I have an Opaque<T>, where that Opaque gets dropped at some point. But the memory backing both Opaque and T itself remains valid. And the T within remains being accessible through different parties (the memory is kept alive through refcounting).

Am I guaranteed that the Rust compiler will not invent any writes to that Opaque?

I have read through the documentations of Opaque, UnsafeCell and MaybeUninit and none of them seem to make any guarantee regarding what happens after drop().
