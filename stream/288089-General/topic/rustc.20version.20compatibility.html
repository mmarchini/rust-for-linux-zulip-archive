In general, old commits and tags will not work with compilers that were in the future. Also, please note that old tags are not supported (i.e. by the kernel in general, not just us).

Instead, what is supported are stable kernel branches, and for those we try our best to keep them working with the newer Rust compilers that get released.

Miguel Ojeda (Mar 20 2025 at 23:41):

No worries, and you're welcome!

I am not sure exactly what you mean, but unstable features can be used even if they are stabilized. The major issue is really changes to those features that could break the kernel (or even removal of features).

We hedge against that by having the kernel in upstream Rust's CI -- so all PRs landing there are required to be pass a minimal kernel build of the Rust parts.

Moreover, we are collaborating with upstream Rust, so they know what we are doing; and anyway we don't allow random features to be enabled. In other words, it is a controlled set, and especially in terms of language features, it is getting very small now.

Even then, if something breaks, we can still workaround things etc. on our side, and we have some weeks until a nightly becomes beta and then a stable release. We test those on our side, and sometimes send preemptive cleanups even for things like Clippy.

I talk a bit about this in e.g. LPC 2024 and FOSDEM 2025.

For stable kernels, we backport whatever fixes or workarounds are needed from mainline, just like anything else. Of course, if one picks an old tag from those, then it may or may not work, just like if one picks an old mainline tag. But old tags are not supported anyway by the stable kernel team, i.e. one is not supposed to use them.

I hope that clarifies!
