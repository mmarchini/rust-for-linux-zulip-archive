Topic: Snake case for register names for driver register structs

Joel Fernandes (Apr 25 2025 at 00:31):

Hello,
We are having a bit of a dilemma with how to proceed with register naming for our Nova driver:

We are register names like this which are quite long and in screaming snake case across our code bases, firmware and hardware references:
NV_PGC6_AON_SECURE_SCRATCH_GROUP_05_PRIV_LEVEL_MASK

Considering this, it is a bit unacceptable to have to define a register like this in Rust "NvPgc6AonSecureScratchGroup05PrivLevelMask".

Can we just define the register directly in the register! macro using the snake case NV_PGC6_AON_SECURE_SCRATCH_GROUP_05_PRIV_LEVEL_MASK ?

So it would look like:
register!(NV_PGC6_AON_SECURE_SCRATCH_GROUP_05_PRIV_LEVEL_MASK@0x00000100,
"Basic revision information about the chip";
3:0 minor_rev => as u8, "minor revision of the chip";
7:4 major_rev => as u8, "major revision of the chip");

Here is a thread for more context: https://lore.kernel.org/all/108225ba-5d8c-459b-97aa-6b57affd5b87@nvidia.com/

John Hubbard (Apr 25 2025 at 00:52):

Let me just pile on here a little bit: first of all, I love the term, "screaming snake case", it's just so vivid and joyful. :)

But more to the point, this really does seem valuable: there are countless hundreds of thousands of lines of these register and field definitions, all from our hardware headers. And we have thousands of engineers who communicate with these names. It would be really helpful to not lose the names.

So somewhere in the Rust code, I think we want to have NV_PGC6_AON_SECURE_SCRATCH_GROUP_05_PRIV_LEVEL_MASK. Perhaps Joel is on the right track, but I defer now to the Rust for Linux experts.

Alexandre Courbot (Apr 25 2025 at 01:08):

Each register has its own type so we can implement its accessor methods - doing so would mean having types defined in SNAKE_CASE, unless we resort to some macro to make things more readable from the consuming modules

Alexandre Courbot (Apr 25 2025 at 01:10):

What we could do is, keep the type names as they are in the regs module, but have all other modules access the registers through some macro that converts the names at compile-time. So the types would still technically be CamelCase, but the consuming code would use SNAKE_CASE.

@John Hubbard would that work for you?

Alexandre Courbot (Apr 25 2025 at 01:11):

that macro would also be local to nova so other drivers can decide how they want to manage this. But yeah, register naming is traditionally strongly biased towards SNAKE_CASE

I think it's reasonable to turn off naming convention warnings for such cases. Keeping consistency with other cases is useful.

Miguel Ojeda (Apr 25 2025 at 10:50):

Lints are just that, lints -- if they really get in the way of a better solution in one way or another, then they can be disabled. For instance, we already disable those in the bindings crate, and this would be a similar case. And it sounds to me like you are thinking of introducing a workaround just to deal with the lint, so I wouldn't recommend that. Nevertheless, of course, the more local / narrower you can keep the expect, the better.
