Miguel Ojeda (Aug 10 2025 at 22:06):

what ongoing projects could benefit from additional programmers / review? Is the issue tracker still the best place to find a way to contribute? What is the best way to get involved without duplicating work?

Essentially all patches sent to the rust-for-linux mailing list would appreciate reviews! :) Reviews are very welcome and very needed, and they are a good way to get to know the code around. So I would suggest subscribing to the mailing list and taking a look at what people submit, or looking in Lore for the latest threads: https://lore.kernel.org/rust-for-linux/

As for contributing, in general, you may want to take a look at the https://rust-for-linux.com/contributing page for other ideas and suggestions.

I hope that helps.

Miguel Ojeda (Aug 14 2025 at 09:03):

For networking, @FUJITA Tomonori , @Trevor Gross, @Valentin Obst et al. may be able to give you some ideas.

In general, if you want to upstream a driver, the easiest path is to find something that does not exist for C yet. Alternatively, you may want to port an existing one, but then the relevant maintainers will need to be on board with it, i.e. in general the kernel does not allow duplicated drivers.

But please also take a look at these links -- I have there some more context there:

https://rust-for-linux.com/contributing#submitting-new-abstractions-and-modules
https://rust-for-linux.com/rust-reference-drivers

I would also suggest searching in Lore for recent threads about the topics you are interested in: https://lore.kernel.org/rust-for-linux/

I hope that helps!
