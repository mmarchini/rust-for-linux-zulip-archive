Gary Guo (Jan 28 2026 at 16:29):

When you call remove, you shouldn't call move_next again, otherwise you're skipping over an element

Alice Ryhl (Jan 28 2026 at 17:03):

A cursor always rests between two elements in the list. This means that a cursor has a previous and next element, but no current element. It also means that it’s possible to have a cursor into an empty list.

https://rust.docs.kernel.org/kernel/list/struct.Cursor.html

Alice Ryhl (Jan 28 2026 at 17:24):

You may think of the List cursor as like the text cursor from when you write text. What you're doing is like pressing <DELETE><RIGHT ARROW>, which will go from A|BCD -> A|CD -> AC|D
