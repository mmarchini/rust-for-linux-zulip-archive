Matt Gilbride (May 30 2023 at 15:08):

Martin Rodriguez Reboredo said:

An initial comment from mine would be to rename RBTree::try_insert to RBTree::try_create_node or RBTree::try_insert_key_val because it'd disambiguate with RBTree::insert as that one inserts an RBTreeNode rather than a key-value pair.

Hm...I'm fine with whatever others think :smile:

My gut reaction is that try_insert_key_val might be verbose if most would understand implicitly that the tree acts as a key/value store. Thus I prefer try_create_node. But if I'm being really pedantic, that name doesn't imply that the node is actually inserted into the tree.

Since try_insert is just a convenience wrapper around try_allocate_node, I guess I'd propose two options:

  1. Remove try_insert altogether. Have clients just use try_allocate_node and insert themselves. Document this pattern.
  2. try_allocate_and_insert
