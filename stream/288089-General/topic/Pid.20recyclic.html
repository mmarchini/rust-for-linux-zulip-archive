Alice Ryhl (May 31 2024 at 18:16):

Does taking a refcount on a struct pid prevent the numeric pid from getting recycled?

Valentin Obst (May 31 2024 at 21:24):

Afaik (pls double check :) ), struct pid's purpose is to decouple the kernel's notion to identify a process from the numeric values reported to user space (a process could equivalently be uniquely identified by its task_structbut keeping that around after exit wastes too much memory). As such, the numeric values (in possilby many namespaces) will be freed on exit()if no tasks are using it anymore (see call paths leading to free_pid, which is where numeric values are given back to ns->idrs), i.e., a ref to struct pidwill not prevent their recycling. I guess another way to see that would be to note that put_pid has no code to mark the numeric values as reusable when the refcount drops to 0.
