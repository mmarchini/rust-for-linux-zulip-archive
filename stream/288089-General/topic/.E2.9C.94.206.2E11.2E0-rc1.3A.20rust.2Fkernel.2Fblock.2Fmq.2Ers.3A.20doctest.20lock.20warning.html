Alice Ryhl (Aug 12 2024 at 12:40):

So, the way lockdep works is that every time you take more than one lock at the time, it records their class and which order you took them in.

Alice Ryhl (Aug 12 2024 at 12:40):

E.g. if you have mutex A with class AC and mutex B with class BC, then locking A and then B at the same time records AC,BC.

Alice Ryhl (Aug 12 2024 at 12:41):

If you later lock B and then A, then it records BC,AC.

Alice Ryhl (Aug 12 2024 at 12:41):

This triggers a lockdep error because AC,BC and BC,AC have the opposite ordering.

Alice Ryhl (Aug 12 2024 at 12:42):

And you must always lock things in the same order to avoid deadlocks.

Andreas Hindborg (Aug 12 2024 at 12:42):

Got it, thanks.

Alice Ryhl (Aug 12 2024 at 12:43):

If A1 and A2 both have class AC, then locking A1 and A2 at the same time also always results in an error.

Alice Ryhl (Aug 12 2024 at 12:43):

Since they have the same class.

Alice Ryhl (Aug 12 2024 at 12:43):

You can reuse lockdep classes as long as none of the above cases results in lockdep warnings.
