Miguel Ojeda (May 07 2024 at 09:11):

I haven't found any more recent work in it, is that just because none is interested in doing the work or are there any known blockers?

Possibly the former. The support we had in arm worked and booted (in QEMU) for a given config/CPU as a proof of concept (armv6). It would be great if someone interested in ARM can review/extend the support if needed, test it with actual hardware (as Trevor said) and submit it upstream (ideally with several configs/variations, say, BE vs. LE).

Miguel Ojeda (May 07 2024 at 09:11):

32-bit x86 is blocked on a couple specific things but I don't know if those apply to ARM

If you mean regparm and reg-struct-return, I don't think they are used in arm32. But it could very well be that other arch-specific flags are needed for particular configs that rustc may not support yet, as you say. It would already be very welcome work if someone can take a look and have an updated report on what may be missing specifically for arm32 (and create/link the issues in/to upstream Rust).
