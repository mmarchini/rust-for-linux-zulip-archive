John Hubbard (Jan 13 2026 at 23:48):

Hi Gary! Some notes: future GPUs won't need this, because we are moving (as requested) to a story in which the GPU itself "knows" how to process the entire ELF image, without intervention by Nova or anything else. I realize that's not what you are asking, though, so:

For these older chips, we were looking at splitting out the things that we are currently parsing out of the ELF, but I've actually forgotten what we decided. That's key, so maybe @Timur Tabi can remind me.

Alexandre Courbot (Jan 14 2026 at 12:39):

should we actually have proper abstractions for handling ELFs and share it with all drivers?

We tried that approach a while ago and it was rejected by Greg KH, hence our move in future firmware to not unpack ELF binaries in the kernel.

Gary Guo (Jan 14 2026 at 14:33):

However more and more features are added this to-be-removed ELF parser, like the ability to do it for 32-bit images, right?

John Hubbard (Jan 14 2026 at 21:06):

One GPU arch required 64-bit ELF firmware, and another one added 32-bit ELF firmware, and now we are All Done.

John Hubbard (Jan 14 2026 at 21:08):

Any additional changes should be for future GPUs, which will be increasingly simpler to deal with.

Future: I'm quite excited to eventually get to the point of "request_firmware(), then poke a register, DONE". The firmware team working on that.

Alexandre Courbot (Jan 15 2026 at 10:30):

The 32-bit support requirement is unfortunate. The firmware could have been designed around 64-bit alone, but since that's how it is released we need to support it for now. However both 64-bit and 32-bit ELF will go away in a (hopefully soon to be) future release of the firmware, so the plan is still to remove the whole code altogether.
