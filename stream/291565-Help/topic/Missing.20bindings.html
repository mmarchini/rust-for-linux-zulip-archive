Leon Gross (Apr 12 2022 at 07:54):

Hello, I currently work on creating an adequate abstraction for the crypto API.
Therefore I analyzed how hashing is performed in C (crypto_shash).
The common way of hashing a value with a salt in C is calling crypto_shash_init() -> crypto_shash_update (n times) -> crypto_shash_final.
I added the <crypto/hash.h>header to the bindgen helpers to include.
I looked through the bindings_generated.rs and realized, that the function crypto_shash_init() is missing.

How does it come that bindgen does not include all bindings?
Also the function crypto_free_shash is missing.

bjorn3 (Apr 12 2022 at 08:10):

@Leon Gross crypto_shash_init is static and inline. This means it doesn't end up being exported by any object file. For this reason bindgen skips it. You can add a wrapper function to the binding helpers called rust_crypto_shash_init and then call this function instead.

Leon Gross (Apr 12 2022 at 08:16):

@bjorn3 thank you for your fast answer! This makes totally sense, I will do it like you proposed.
