Andreas Hindborg (Jun 05 2025 at 07:15):

Kernel can't find your root file system. Maybe you are missing root= or you did not compile in or load the module required to get to your rootfs.

Andreas Hindborg (Jun 05 2025 at 07:18):

But the general question is a good one, with many answers. I think every one has their own workflow more or less. But there are tools to help as well. One very popular one is virtme-ng. It boots your kernel in qemu and mounts your host filesystem read-only inside the VM. It then adds overlayfs on top so you can do ephemeral writes as well.

Andreas Hindborg (Jun 05 2025 at 07:20):

I'd like to also promote my own solution, run-kernel. It boots your kernel with a NixOS userland by mounting the nix store from the host into your VM via virtio-fs. So similarly to virtme-ng you don't have to build a disk image. But unlike virtme-ng, you can have a different system inside the VM than outside. You simply describe the system with a nix expression, and you will get that system booted in your VM.

That depends a lot on what you are doing and the subsystem (e.g. writing a data structure vs. testing with actual hardware etc.).
