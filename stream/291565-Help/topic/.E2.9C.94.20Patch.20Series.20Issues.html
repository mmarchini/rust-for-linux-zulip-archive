Alice Ryhl (Jan 31 2026 at 11:54):

  1. It's because they are missing the In-Reply-To: header. Each patch should reference the message-id of the cover letter here.
  2. Just in cover letter is fine. But in your case the commit hash is meaningless because it's the commit hash of your local copy of the pending commit (I'm guessing).
  3. Just [PATCH] is fine, but you should say in the cover letter what you depend on. E.g., with a lore link to the patch it depends on.
  4. One should generally wait at least a few days between new versions, but the "this series was sent incorrectly" case is an exception. There it's ok to immediately send a v2 (or just resend v1), and then leave a reply on the broken version that you resent it.
  5. See above.

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 31 2026 at 12:01):
