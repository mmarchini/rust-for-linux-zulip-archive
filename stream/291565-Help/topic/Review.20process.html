While we are at it, I want to bring up another "pain point" in the review process: sometimes reviewers may go too deep into the details while the whole design may still have a problem. This doesn't just happen to our project, I can see it everywhere. But Rust and our development principles have a very high standard (that is a good thing), and as a result refactoring an amostly-done PR is usually much more challenging than C. Fundamentally since types are propositions and functions are proofs, so changing the desgin means (most of the time) changing the propositions (things that the code guaratees), and changing the propositions means changing the types, and Rust's system can express a lot more than C's.

To avoid the effect of a "last minute's design change", maybe we can split our review process into two stages: type/design review stage and code/implementation review stage?

  • type/design review stage: reviewers focus on the overall design (around types), care about things like "whether this type should be Send/Unpin?", "what's the invariants of the type?" and "do the methods of type provide safe and ergonomic interface to users?"
  • code/implementation review stage: reviewers focus on implementation, documentation, coding style, etc. Somehing found in this stage may make us go back to change something we decide in type review, but it should be rare.

It doesn't need to have two clearly separate stages, and it's rather a consensus between reviewers and contributors and maybe a Type-Reviewed-by tag. Other advantages I can think of:

  • Provides contributors a better experience, at least this could answer the question: how much confidence I have to use the interface of this PR to my next PR.
  • Gains more reviewers, once a PR is type reviewed, then people that are interested in this project but don't know where to start can jump in (fearlessly ;-)). It's another way to get yourself started.

To summarize, I think design review should be the first step of reviewing a PR, especially given the strength of Rust type system.
