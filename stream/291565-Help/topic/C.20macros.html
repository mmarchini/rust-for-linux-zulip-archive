My understanding is that bindgen does not translate macros, therefore if we wanted to use something like virt_mb() or barrier() is there somewhere those are available? If they aren't where should I place them? Rust supports in-line assembly so I could just have my own macro, but I think something like virt_mb() would be something others would also want.

Alex Gaynor (Jun 18 2021 at 20:13):

Take a look at helpers.c -- we re-expose macros there as regular functions.

Wei Liu (Jun 19 2021 at 10:10):

The problem with exporting a macro like virt_mb or barrier to regular functions will be too heavy weight. We don't want to pay for the extra function call overhead.

Miguel Ojeda (Jun 19 2021 at 10:17):

Cross-language LTO should mostly take care of that.

Miguel Ojeda (Jun 19 2021 at 10:17):

But yeah, eventually, it is likely we may end up with some duplicate functionality to improve that.

Miguel Ojeda (Jun 19 2021 at 10:18):

However, for the moment, I think avoiding duplicate implementations on both sides is best.
