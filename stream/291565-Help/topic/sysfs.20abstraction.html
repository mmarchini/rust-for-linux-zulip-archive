Greg KH (Apr 21 2025 at 07:03):

That's a copy of my 2013 blog post about this topic which is here http://kroah.com/log/blog/2013/06/26/how-to-create-a-sysfs-file-correctly/

That's kind of close, you shouldn't be wanting to create a "raw" sysfs abstraction, the api should be going through the bus, as it is the bus-type that your driver is dealing with, and is handling the sysfs files for.

The only part of the kernel that should be dealing with "raw" sysfs files right now is filesystems, and for them, someone really needs to just take the time and write a proper abstraction so that they don't have to do that. We never expected filesystems to want to touch sysfs when it was created, so we kind of just ignored that portion of the kernel :)

Greg KH (Apr 25 2025 at 14:37):

driver_attribute should hopefully never be needed, the in-kernel use of it is very small and should really be revisited again as it's almost always a bad idea (options/attributes should be on a per-device level, not a per-driver level)

device_attribute is needed, of course, but again, that ties into the platform_driver api, and overall the driver core apis, that is not a "raw" sysfs interaction (i.e. with a struct kobject).
