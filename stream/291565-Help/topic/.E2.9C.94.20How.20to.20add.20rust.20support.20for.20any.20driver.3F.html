Fabien Parent (Jun 19 2024 at 17:44):

@wenzhao: Rust support in the kernel is still very new, so many things are missing, including step by step guides. Most people here are getting started by reading the code from other people from the Mailing List or from their development branches.
Since most of the things for driver development are not upstream, Rust APIs can change quickly and it would not be useful to have a step by step guide for getting started with driver development. Though as the Device Driver core abstraction is getting merged, there is hope that more things goes in soon and things stabilize.

If you need some inspiration about some "simple" device drivers with the subsystem abstractions needed, I have a couple of branches that you can look into that might help you get started:

  • https://github.com/Fabo/linux/commits/fparent/rust-ncv6336/
  • https://github.com/Fabo/linux/commits/fparent/rust-truly-r63350

For bindgen in particular, you can look at this I2C commit in the first branch I shared: https://github.com/Fabo/linux/commit/8bf3e991e9f15a85560f001f56ef25ea814c0fc6
