Miguel Ojeda (Jan 15 2025 at 09:13):

I am not sure what you mean -- you can use/develop against old versions on your own (as long as you respect the license etc.). It is just that older kernel versions (apart from the stable ones) are not supported, so you cannot upstream code or get bugs fixed.

But if what you are asking is whether you can rely on APIs to remain as-is, then: internal kernel APIs are not stable (this applies to all the kernel, not just Rust APIs), please read https://docs.kernel.org/process/stable-api-nonsense.html
