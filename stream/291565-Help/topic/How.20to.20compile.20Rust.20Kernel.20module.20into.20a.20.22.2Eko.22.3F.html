To clarify, that is an out-of-tree module, which is possible to use, but I normally suggest developing in-tree.

To do it in-tree, you don't need to replace an existing module, you can simply add a new one by mimicking what the others do. As @Dirk Behme mentioned, looking at the samples is an easy way to get started. You will need to modify the Kconfig and Makefile files in the folder, plus adding a new .rs file.
