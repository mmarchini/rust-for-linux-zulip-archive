Miguel Ojeda (Apr 11 2023 at 10:28):

Andreas Hindborg said:

So if I went ahead and enabled the btree in our alloc crate and added fallible allocation methods, that would be upstreamable to the kernel? That would mean that we diverge from Rust alloc, and it is not certain that Rust will accept our fallible btree additions.

It would be technically upstreamable, i.e. there is nothing preventing us from doing so, including licensing. I was confused why you asked because we already have alloc in-tree, and it was of course checked that the licensing was OK a long time ago.

Now, whether we should upstream additions or not is a different question. For fallible methods, adding those is within scope, since that is why we added our fork to begin with. If it is other kinds of methods, or adding our GFP flags, etc., that is a different story, because that is likely not upstreamable to Rust, and we need to think about what will happen there (given the recent developments around the other try_* methods and other discussions).
