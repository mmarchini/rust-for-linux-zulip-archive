Konstantin Andrikopoulos (Nov 22 2024 at 16:50):

As a follow up to this. If the current version of the patch has been reviewed by a reviewer, but someone else made some comments I would like to address in a new version, do I need to preserve the previous review? Or just send the new version?

Miguel Ojeda (Nov 22 2024 at 17:06):

If you mean preserving their "Reviewed-by" tag, then it depends on whether the changes are substantial enough that the original review does not apply anymore.

Miguel Ojeda (Nov 22 2024 at 17:07):

For instance, when someone spots a typo in documentation, it does not invalidate a "Reviewed-by" made by someone else.

Miguel Ojeda (Nov 22 2024 at 17:07):

But if you rework part of the patch in a way that you think it needs a re-review, then you should delete the tag in the new version.

Miguel Ojeda (Nov 22 2024 at 17:10):

When in doubt, the safest is to delete the tag and request it again, possibly below the --- line, e.g. "Out of an abundance of caution, I removed the Reviewed-by tag -- could you please re-review it? Thanks!"
