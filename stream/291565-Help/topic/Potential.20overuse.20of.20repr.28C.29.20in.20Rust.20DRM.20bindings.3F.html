Topic: Potential overuse of repr(C) in Rust DRM bindings?

Rahul Rameshbabu (Aug 16 2025 at 18:46):

I was reading through the Rust DRM bindings since I am working on some new functionality there. I was a bit surprised by the following pattern.

/// A typed DRM device with a specific `drm::Driver` implementation.
///
/// The device is always reference-counted.
///
/// # Invariants
///
/// `self.dev` is a valid instance of a `struct device`.
#[repr(C)]
#[pin_data]
pub struct Device<T: drm::Driver> {
    dev: Opaque<bindings::drm_device>,
    #[pin]
    data: T::Data,
}

bindings::drm_device is already #repr(C), and I do not understand the value of making drm::Device #repr(C). If I understand correctly, it is not being based through the FFI boundaries?

Alice Ryhl (Aug 17 2025 at 07:58):

It has the effect of ensuring that dev is placed before data.

Daniel Almeida (Aug 17 2025 at 11:24):

In particular, IIRC, we need to enforce the C layout if we want container_of & friends to work

Benno Lossin (Aug 17 2025 at 12:56):

container_of! also works with repr(Rust) structs

Rahul Rameshbabu (Aug 17 2025 at 22:07):

Alice Ryhl said:

It has the effect of ensuring that dev is placed before data.

Hmm, that was my initial thought. However as pointed out by this thread, I would expect offset_of!, container_of! , etc. to all work fine with Rust representation without needing to ensure the memory layout in a way where the declared field order decides the memory layout. At least, this is the conclusion I have come to from my skim of rust/kernel/drm/device.rs. However, maybe I am missing some logic that requires this ordering.

Janne Grunau (Aug 18 2025 at 13:07):

it's not fully insulated from the FFI border as its memory allocated/freed on C side through __drm_dev_alloc. That should work with repr(Rust) the offset of bindings::drm_device is passed as argument and the struct itself isn't directly used on C side.
repr(C) could have limited use when looking at register dumps of kernel warnings/oopses
