Miguel Ojeda (Jun 16 2023 at 12:29):

I guess that's additional work

It is part of a maintainer's job, so they are expected to do it, no worries :)

In fact, if you give the explicit Tested-by: ... <...> in your email, then b4 will automatically apply the tag to the commit message, and thus is less work for them than having to decide manually and/or having to ask the tagger if it is unclear if they meant to give the tag or not (that is why b4 is quite nice for maintainers).

From what I notice should I just reply to all patches in the series and add Tested-by tag?

Yeah, ideally you would send it for each one that you tested.

Sometimes tags are given in the cover letter (e.g. Reviewed-by), but for Tested-by in a patch series that fix/improve a few orthogonal issues, it can be ambiguous, so it is best to give it explicitly for each patch that you tested.
