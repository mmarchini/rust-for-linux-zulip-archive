Miguel Ojeda (Oct 03 2024 at 11:54):

I'm unsure whether the commit (or maybe it should be multiple commits?) for this issue should mention more than what is essentially just a "fix the clippy lints"

It should typically explain more, especially for cases where it may not be clear why how to clean it up, i.e. one may apply Clippy's suggestion, or may #[allow] it, or may #[expect] it, or perhaps allow the lint at a module level, or maybe even disable globally the lint if we really do not want it anymore -- but why did you pick the option you picked? That is what the commit should explain.

You can even do one fix per commit, if you feel that is clearer for a reviewer. One per kind of lint is usually useful, even if it fixes several instances of the same lint, to separate discussions.

For instance, please take a look at these examples:

Commit 05cef2c4a421 ("rust: kunit: use C-string literals to clean warning")
Commit 7bc186731e87 ("rust: error: allow useless_conversion for 32-bit builds")
Commit 935c4833426f ("rust: rbtree: fix SAFETY comments that should be # Safety sections")
