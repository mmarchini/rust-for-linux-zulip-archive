Miguel Ojeda (Jul 15 2025 at 11:38):

No, you shouldn't add unstable features or try to build core.o as a single-target.

What you want to do is modify rust/Makefile to mimic what is done for e.g. the ffi crate (which is one that goes after compiler_builtins).

From a quick look though, you seem to need proc macros, syn and so on. If that is the case, then it will be more complex -- you can take a look at e.g. this prototype I wrote back then: https://github.com/Rust-for-Linux/linux/pull/1007.

I hope that helps -- good luck!

Miguel Ojeda said:

From a quick look though, you seem to need proc macros, syn and so on. If that is the case, then it will be more complex

Actually, I've managed to make my safety_macro work on kernel crates, without pulling in any proc-macro related source code, but rather relying on rust binary libs.

The idea is to precompile the proc macro to a libsafety_macro.so, and compile safety_lib based on libsafety_macro.so, core, and compiler_builtins to libsafety_lib.rlib. And then pass these dependencies flags through safety-tool-rfl a rustc wrapper

-L/home/gh-zjp-CN/tag-std/safety-tool/safety-lib # containing libsafety_macro.so
--extern=safety=/home/gh-zjp-CN/tag-std/safety-tool/safety-lib/libsafety_lib.rlib

# some extra tool attr flags as well
-Zcrate-attr=feature(register_tool)
-Zcrate-attr=register_tool(rapx)

so #[safety::precond::Property] is just avaliable in each kernel crate module.
