Jesung Yang (Nov 20 2025 at 21:10):

This is because sample-mod.rs is not registered in rust-project.json. The easiest way to just simply make it work is to move your sample-mod.rs into somewhere already registered (e.g., samples/rust/), and modify the Makefile (e.g., samples/rust/Makefile) to target your object file. Then just make LLVM=1 rust-anaylzer again.

You can check scripts/generated_rust_analyzer.py to see which folders are registered:

    # Then, the rest outside of `rust/`.
    #
    # We explicitly mention the top-level folders we want to cover.
    extra_dirs = map(lambda dir: srctree / dir, ("samples", "drivers"))

Jesung Yang (Nov 20 2025 at 21:50):

The simplest change would be something like this:

diff --git a/samples/rust/Makefile b/samples/rust/Makefile
index cf8422f8f219..1b524eed7601 100644
--- a/samples/rust/Makefile
+++ b/samples/rust/Makefile
@@ -13,6 +13,7 @@ obj-$(CONFIG_SAMPLE_RUST_DRIVER_USB)          += rust_driver_usb.o
 obj-$(CONFIG_SAMPLE_RUST_DRIVER_FAUX)          += rust_driver_faux.o
 obj-$(CONFIG_SAMPLE_RUST_DRIVER_AUXILIARY)     += rust_driver_auxiliary.o
 obj-$(CONFIG_SAMPLE_RUST_CONFIGFS)             += rust_configfs.o
+obj-m          += sample-mod.o

 rust_print-y := rust_print_main.o rust_print_events.o

This will build sample-mod.rs as a module.
