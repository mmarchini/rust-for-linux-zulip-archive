Hi all,

I recently submitted this patch to the kernel and @Alice Ryhl pointed me that there are merge conflicts with other patchsets.

So I have two questions:

  1. How do we resolve conflict issues? Do I wait for other patchsets to be merged and push a v2?
  2. From what origin should I fork and submit my changes? Rust-for-linux rust-next branch or Torvalds master branch?

Alice Ryhl (Jan 07 2024 at 12:52):

In principle, the answer is just that Miguel will fix the merge conflict when he takes your patch.

Alice Ryhl (Jan 07 2024 at 12:52):

Of course, it's helpful for him if the patches are sent in a way that avoids them.

Alice Ryhl (Jan 07 2024 at 12:52):

In this case, you could have based it on rust-next and avoid one conflict.

Charalampos Mitrodimas (Jan 07 2024 at 12:57):

Understood, thanks.

Would it be helpful for Miguel to submit a v2 with the patch based on rust-next?

Alice Ryhl (Jan 07 2024 at 13:00):

Most likely. If you get some review that requires you to make some other change as well, then it would definitely make sense to base v2 on rust-next. But if you don't otherwise need to send a v2, then it's not a requirement that you do it (but you still can). You also have to balance it with the fact that you should give people a chance to comment on v1 before you send v2.
