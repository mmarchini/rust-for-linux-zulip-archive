Onur Özkan (Jul 16 2026 at 09:49):

Is there one self-contained document that explains the whole process: where to find possible work, how to check that nobody is already working on it, and where to announce that you intend to take it?

Not really. The closest thing to a self-contained guide is:

  • https://github.com/Rust-for-Linux/linux/issues/2
  • https://github.com/Rust-for-Linux/linux/issues/1058

but even those can't cover everything simply because each tree has its own way of planning and organizing work independently. There isn't a single page that tracks the plans or ongoing work across all trees and maintainers.

I’m not looking for someone to hand me a specific beginner issue. I’m looking for a repeatable guide that is complete enough for a newcomer to follow without already knowing which sources to check.

I think the best approach is to follow the zulip and mailing list discussions and look for opportunities where you can help. As far as I know that's how most maintainers got started (with IRC instead of zulip for some of them). There isn't a simple, repeatable process that covers everything you are looking for.
