Gary Guo (Jan 30 2026 at 15:09):

klint needs to identify special items to perform linting. An example is that it needs to which function is the build_error so it can check with it. Currently, I have a hack baiscally hard-coded the kernel implementation detail into klint: https://github.com/Rust-for-Linux/klint/blob/trunk/src/diagnostic_items/out_of_band.rs.

@Miguel Ojeda do you think I should keep doing this, or would it be okay to also add attributes like #[klint::diagnostic_item = "build_error"] around special items?

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 30 2026 at 17:54):

I think it would be fine -- that is how I would have thought about it, in fact.

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 30 2026 at 17:55):

Perhaps some may find it jarring, but it would seem to me it would allow us to make changes on the kernel side more freely, i.e. without breaking klint (even if we typically don't change that sort of thing).

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 30 2026 at 17:55):

And for things that require lists of identifiers, i.e. not just a single one, it is even more important, I would say.

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 30 2026 at 17:56):

i.e. personally I think it is way better to mark in our source files where we want a lint to apply, very close to the actual item, rather than maintain a global list somewhere else (that then would need to be versioned as well and so on and so forth).

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 30 2026 at 17:57):

I suggested a similar thing for Clippy and its config for lints that require passing a list of things to match.

Miguel Ojeda (Jan 30 2026 at 17:58):

Same could apply for bindgen's lists of items in flags.
